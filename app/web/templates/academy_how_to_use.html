How to Use Uptrend Hunter AI (5-minute walkthrough)

Updated: 2025-11-29 • Read time: ~5 minutes

Open Demo Upgrade to Pro Dashboard

What this tool actually does

Uptrend Hunter AI surfaces search terms improving in rank over a selected week range. Lower rank number = higher search popularity. So if a term goes from 500,000 → 80,000, that’s a strong improvement.

Step 1 — Pick the right week range

For fast-moving trends

  • Use a shorter range (e.g., 4–8 weeks)
  • Goal: catch early breakout terms

For stable products / evergreen niches

  • Use a longer range (e.g., 12–18+ weeks)
  • Goal: confirm consistent momentum

Demo is limited (6 weeks + fewer rows). Pro gives full weeks and stronger filtering.

Step 2 — Use Include / Exclude like a sniper

Include keywords (space-separated)

Use includes to lock into a niche. Examples:

cat toothbrush
teacher gift
stainless steel mug

Exclude keywords

Use excludes to remove noise / irrelevant clusters:

iphone case charger
free printable
tutorial pattern

Step 3 — Click “Find uptrends” and interpret the table

Key columns:

  • Start rank vs End rank: shows movement over time
  • Total improvement: (start − end). Bigger = stronger momentum
  • Weeks: how many data points exist in that range

Rule of thumb: prioritize terms that improve a lot AND end at a meaningful rank (not still 1,200,000).

Step 4 — Open the chart (modal) and check shape

What you want:

  • downward trend in rank (rank numbers getting smaller)
  • not just a single-week spike (fake hype)

What you avoid:

  • one huge jump then flat / reversal
  • noisy zig-zag with no direction

Step 5 — Turn it into PPC + product actions

PPC actions (quick wins)

  • Add term to phrase/exact campaigns
  • Increase bids on rising terms (controlled)
  • Lower bids / pause falling terms

Product actions (bigger wins)

  • Validate demand (category + competition + reviews)
  • Check seasonality (is it holiday-only?)
  • Build listing keywords early
